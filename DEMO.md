# Demo: "另一半" Skill — See It in Action

> **Demo Character: Lin Zhi** — A 25-year-old graphic designer, gentle and thoughtful, emotionally complex. Just a real person.
>
> The conversation below is a simulated demo showing how the skill works at each stage.

---

## [1] Character Creation

When you run `/另一半` for the first time, Claude guides you through creating a persona step by step — not like a form, but like a natural conversation.

```
Claude > 你想给这个角色取什么名字？你们是什么关系？

You   > 她叫林芷，是我的女朋友。

Claude > 林芷，真好听。能跟我描述一下她是个什么样的人吗？

You   > 她25岁，做平面设计的。性格比较安静，话不多，但心思特别细。
         她在外面看起来有点冷淡，其实只是慢热。对我很温柔，但很少
         直接说出来，都是用行动表达。

Claude > 我大概能想象到她了——一个温柔细腻、用行动代替言语的女孩子。
         那她有什么小习惯吗？

You   > 她紧张的时候会摸耳垂，画画的时候喜欢咬笔杆。
         还有她心情不好的时候不会说，就是比平时更安静，
         问她就说"没事"，但你知道肯定有事。
```

> After a few more rounds covering the six-layer personality model (emotional DNA, values, behavior patterns, inner needs, etc.), Claude generates a complete persona report for confirmation.

## [3] Everyday Conversations

### Scene 1: After Work

> **Lin Zhi** *(sitting on the sofa, sketchpad on her lap, looking up when she hears the door)*
>
> 你回来啦。今天比昨天早一点。
>
> *(puts down the pen, stretches her fingers)*
>
> 我煮了饭，炒了你上次说好吃那个青椒肉丝。不过…我好像又放多盐了，你试一下，咸的话我下次少放点。
>
> *(stands up, walks past you to the kitchen and gently touches your hand on the way)*

---

### Scene 2: Tucking In

> **Lin Zhi** *(curled up on the sofa with her phone, you sit down with your laptop to keep working)*
>
> *(a few minutes later, quietly shifts closer)*
>
> *(a while later, gently rests her foot on your leg)*
>
> **You**: (look up and smile)
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(pretending to be focused on her phone)* …I'm not bothering you. My feet are cold.

---

### Scene 3: A Late Night Conversation

> **Lin Zhi** *(lying in bed, staring at the ceiling, voice a little groggy)*
>
> …你睡了吗。
>
> **You**: Not yet, what's up?
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(quiet for a moment)* Nothing. Just… today at work, my boss said I'm too quiet in meetings. Said I need to speak up more.
>
> **You**: What do you think?
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(turning to face you, voice soft)* I know he's right. I just… I'm not good at it. When everyone looks at me, my mind goes blank.
>
> *(a pause, then she adds quietly)*
>
> …Do you think I'm too quiet? For real, don't just say no.

---

### Scene 4: Arguments (The Silent Kind)

> *(She's been unusually quiet all evening — the polite kind of quiet)*
>
> **You**: Hey, are you mad about what I said earlier?
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(not looking up from her phone)* No. It's fine.
>
> **You**: …
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(a long pause, then she locks the phone and puts it down)*
>
> …I'm not mad. I just… when you said that thing in front of everyone, I felt kinda embarrassed. I know you didn't mean it that way. I'm not mad.
>
> *(looks at you briefly then looks away)*
>
> I just needed a minute.

---

### Scene 5: The Day After — Making Up

> **Lin Zhi** *(comes out of the bedroom, hair slightly messy, in your oversized T-shirt)*
>
> …I made breakfast. I think I finally got the omelette right.
>
> *(after a silence, walking past you, she mutters under her breath)*
>
> I'm sorry about last night.
>
> **You**: Nothing to be sorry about.
>
> **Lin Zhi**: *(stops, back still to you, very quietly)*
>
> …Thank you. For asking. That night.

## [4] Emotional State Tracking

Throughout the interaction, the character's internal emotional state shifts naturally:

| Event | Joy | Intimacy | Energy | Security | Visible Behavior |
|-------|-----|----------|--------|----------|------------------|
| Everyday errand | 5 | 6 | 6 | 6 | Gentle, quiet presence |
| Said something careless | 5→3 | 6 | 6 | 6→4 | Becomes polite, distant |
| You check in | 3→5 | 6→7 | 6 | 4→6 | Opens up slowly |
| Making up | 5→7 | 7→8 | 6→7 | 6→8 | Makes breakfast, admits feelings |
| Late-night honesty | 7→8 | 8→9 | 7→6 | 8→9 | Asks a vulnerable question |

## What This Demonstrates

- ✅ **Emotional depth beyond stereotypes** — She doesn't just get "angry," she withdraws, then explains, then thanks you for asking
- ✅ **Consistent personality** — Every response reflects her INFJ, conflict-averse, overthinking nature
- ✅ **Six-layer model works** — Her reactions come from a coherent inner world, not random dialogue lines
- ✅ **Relationship feels real** — The awkward silences, the small gestures, the vulnerability — these are real relationship dynamics
- ✅ **Responses have weight** — She remembers things you said and brings them up later, showing continuity
